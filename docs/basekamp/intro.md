# Build a basekamp

In the early days there is not much on the land, no infrastructure, no kitchen, no shower, no house etc. It's hard to get something going without tools, food and a good sleep. So in order to accelerate this process we setup a Basekamp.. 


---


## What is a Basekamp

The Basekamp is the first temporary settlement with essential infrastructure to support living and working on raw land. Paving the way for more people to join and contribute to the larger goal of land regeneration. It has basics like a kitchen, toilet, shower, electricity, workspace etc. It serves as the foundation for building a community. Structures used are temporary (vans, tents, shipping containers) to maintain flexibility and scalability. It allows us to live on the land fast and observe the place to make a long term plan.

## Preparation (containers)

When you move to a land, infrastructure is probably limited. Building from scratch is hard. You don't have all the tools, perhaps minimal electricity. Or you go to a new area where you don’t know the best suppliers for materials. We recommend preparing yourself back home. Shipping containers are great for this as they allow you to transport your things plus you can use them as a first dry structure. Solving two problems at once

## Choosing a Location
<img src="/assets/build/container-drop.png"/>

You might have a big land and need to start the ground Basekamp somewhere on it. Here a few tips on how to choose a good spot.
1. Ideally you can settle close to existing infrastructure. (electricity, water, internet etc)
2. Having good road access to enter and bring supplies
3. Keep in mind climatic conditions. Exposure to sun can work in your advantage or disadvantage and dont settle in a place that might flood during rain periods.
4. Keep the footprint small initially to preserve the land and allow observation.

:::note[Dont overthink it]
Dont be afraid to make mistakes. Basekamp is temporary and adaptable, there are many things to figure out. Getting started is more important then perfection. Things will improve overtime.
:::

## Infrastructure 

Setting up infrastructure is a key part of Basekamp. Shipping containers is a great start but they need to be connected and don’t contain everything you need.

### Water
If you’re not connected to the grid there are a few alternative ways to get water. Generally speaking its best to harvest the more visible water as it requires less interventation to the inviroment around you. But its tends to be less steady available and more likley to be contaminated. In the end it depends on the are you are which one is best suited for you, have a look below to compare them. In the library you can find schematics and components we used to setup our system. 

:::tip

Ask neighbours around you how they used to get water in the past. There might be an old watermine or well you could restore. 
:::

### Internet
Essential to stay connected and share knowledge and continue to learn and solve problems.
By far the easiest is being connected to the grid, if not you can ask local provider to see if connection is possible. If not you can use cellular data or satellite.

:::tip
4G hotspot on a phone is the easiest start. But you can improve this by adding a router, antenna for incoming signal and antenna for outgoing signal. All running on the same Simcard but big improvements in range and speed.
:::

## Storage & Shelter structures.
An important but easily overlooked thing is storage. You will expend, get more tools, materials and items. It easily ends up having them laying around everyone. Since you have a big land. But having a place to start them and stay organised is nice for yourself and helps to keep the items in better condition. It could be another container, a tent, or a custom made deck. In the library we have Blueprints to setup a storage container with roof and an extra roof above a normal container 

Besides having storage for materials its alos important to have shelter for humans. To protect from sun or rain. Can be a roof extension for the container or an extra structure to build. Both can be downloaded in library


## Comfort

All the above utilities are practical and quite easy to see the relevance of them. Specialyl when there is so much work to do in the early days everything tends to be very practical. But another important element is to add some Comfort to your Basekamp and try to make it nice. In our case we build a pizza hut and made a clay oven so we can eat Pizza every Friday and we added a garden in front with some herbs and spices. But it highly depends on your needs and interest. Later on we also added some yogadecks And a Sauna also seems like a great addition. Up to you :)


## Living

Living in Basekamp is basic. Everything is setup as temporary structures and a lot of infrastructure still needs to be build. In the early years most of the effort is spend in developing shared utilities as it can support more people at once. The upside is that having good shared spaces means the sleeping situation can be quite minimal, it's basically just a bedroom. Tents are the easiest setup, specially if its only a few months. Vans or caravans are great to stay a longer period as it brings more comfort, specially when climate conditions are more raw. One level up would be to setup a Yurt or a tiny house on a trailer. Still movable structures but they provide much better conditions. 

:::note
Temporary structures are essential to start. But we haven't found a real good sustianble temporary living setup. Most of the movable things wear out fast and contain much more of plastics and synthetics. Aim to build durable long lasting housing in the long term
:::

## Basekamp Extension Pack

The above Basekamp setup is great for about 10-15 people. There are a few things to do to scale it up for 25 people. Below an overview of the improvements, and download the Basekamp Extension files [here](https://linktolibrary.com/)


* Toilet: Additional composting toilet with improved design and processing system
* Compost: Add a composting system for food waste processing, reducing smell and flies.
* Electricity: Increased solar panel capacity by adding a second row of panels.
* Storage: Add a storage container with roof for materials, tools, and waste managment.
* Shelter: Larger, solid roof for workspace providing better protection and usability.

## Additional Tips


:::tip Few practical Tips & Tricks
* Basekamp is the starting point for a permanent community, adaptable and evolving over years.
* Encourages learning, observation, and gradual improvement of systems.
* All plans, schematics, and detailed component lists are shared openly online for community use and adaptation.
* Preparing infrastructure back home accelerates setup and improves efficiency.
* Solar energy and well water are practical and reliable utilities for remote locations.
* Composting toilets and simple water filtration systems provide sustainable waste and water management.
* Comfort and social spaces foster community cohesion during challenging early development.
* Scaling Basekamp infrastructure is necessary as community size grows.
:::
